Iceland Imps - RustyGoomba

The Iceland Imps played their Week 5 interdivision match against the Troubling Trout and the match went only slightly differently than expected. While the Imps may have struggled by running Syn (Bark) on support, the result was expected from a team that has been running through the Maroon division throughout the entire season. What started with a question of how versatile and effective this team could be became a question of what the possible weak point of this team is, as their innovative comps (ex. 7-man) have led them to an undefeated season and a place as the clear favorite for the winner of the now double-elimination bracket. 


Next Match: vs. Welsh Whales


  1. Pokhara Pusheens - RustyGoomba

While the Pusheens may not have had the flashiest season, they’ve cemented themselves as a top team. They ended Week 5 in a 5 map series against the Arizona O-Teas where they began to stumble but still looked dominant enough in their map wins to remain at the top of the power rankings. The team has steadily improved and had fair consistency, being one of two teams to take a map off the Imps (along with Trout) in the early part of the season and finishing the season without any other match loss. They’re likely to finish in the Top 3, but their lower bracket entrance is not as much in their hands as it is for the team right below them. 


Next Match: vs. Dorado Ducks


  1. Ema Skies - RustyGoomba

The Ema Skies were able to hold off a red hot Phiji Phrogs lineup and kept their composure to finish off Temple of Anubis and win their Week 5 match. Though the team may have faced some difficulties against the Anglers and the Whales, they were able to hold steady and finish with an undefeated season. Their consistency is something to be admired, and they’ve even shown some good compositional flexibility to rotate all of their players in and play around opposing team’s counterpicks. The playoff success of this team is likely going to be dictated by the first 2 matches that they will play in the playoffs, as Season 5 MVP Fancy is gone for the first 3-4 weeks (2-3 matches) of the playoffs. If they can hold off the O-Teas or at least one lower bracket match, then this team is an easy contender for the finals. 


Next Match: vs. Arizona O-Teas


  1. Awesome Anglers - Nuttyr

The Anglers handled the Cats for their final match of the season, winning 3-1 after the team looked somewhat off for the first map, but the tankline really picked it up for maps 2-4, with Sonic and BOON1E running through the Cats. With the win the team locks up the 3rd seed in Maroon division. Although the team is 2-3, they’ve had arguably the hardest schedule in the league this season aside from the 5th seeded Cats or Trout. The team looks best when Bonezee is playing aggressively with space, or flanking for picks, and when the team can survive long enough in drawn out fights for their Dps and Tanks to get more value. The question mark of Mightydwarf’s availability might be an issue, but Zelos has been able to pick up the slack. The team definitely struggled against some of the higher ranked teams, but the match against the Skies looked dead even, with back and forth maps each round. Overall this is a strong team, and with Bonezee on your team anything is possible.


Next Match: vs. Phiji Phrogs


  1. Arizona O-Teas - Nuttyr

The O-Teas looked really good for a few maps against the Pusheens, forcing the Pusheens to go to map 5 for the first time all season. Although this team looked kinda meh early on, getting swept by the Anglers, that match would be much closer today, as the O-Teas have probably shown the most improvement of any team in the league. This team has good comms and the support line has looked really strong all season, with WiggleHiggle showing tons of advancement over the course of the season, and Strud3l having tons of fragging potential while Riddle controls the backline. This team does run comps that can be considerably under sr, but good teammate can make up for that against most teams. I could easily see this team turning some heads come playoffs.


Next Match: vs. Ema Skies


  1. Phiji Phrogs - RustyGoomba

The Phrogs started the season in the Phrog zone due to their incredible carry potential but questionable lineup depth. They end the season in the Phrog zone due to their roster consisting of only 3 players that were on the roster in Week 1, the captain (Wags), the namesake (Phrog), and the original Phrog muffin (MuffinLumps). They were able to take a map off the highly rated Skies in Week 5, but with the trade of Whalefish and Nagaina for Oks and Spacemuffin (Muffin 2.0) going into the playoffs, the ability to properly gauge where this team stands is effectively like the preseason power rankings (completely pointless). Still, a strong core of players ready to carry fights is still within this team, and if they can get some good improvement before they get knocked to the lower bracket they could still be a threatening team to make a deep run. Don’t be surprised wherever they place in the playoffs, but personally I suspect the lack of synergy will bite them before too long.


Next Match: vs. Awesome Anglers

Team of the Week 5


    With the final week of Season 5.5’s regular season wrapped up, we have the final installment of our Team of the Week. With 4 players on this team making their second appearance of the season on Team of the Week, this team contains quite a few performances that were consistent with their play all season long, in addition to some clutch players making their first appearance.


TheNortLogic : Tank

Making his second appearance of the season after Week 1, TheNortLogic came up big for the Whales, helping them get their first victory of the season and narrowly grabbing the final spot in the winner’s bracket for themselves. With 3 notable clutches on Oasis that resulted in point flips due to his stalling, goomba stomping, or bombing on D.Va, the Whales barely missed out on winning map 1, but forced every round to go down to the wire. That is not to mention his consistent play throughout the other maps, as he played a huge part in the Whales comeback victory.


Sonicronic : Tank

One look at Sonic’s stats on Sigma this week is all it took to earn him a spot on this team. In conjunction with BOON1E on Reinhardt, the Angler’s tank line this week was a force to be reckoned with. At one point on Blizzard World, Sonic’s Sigma was out damaging the enemy tankline by himself, in addition to each of his own Dps. On D.Va he had some huge multikill bombs throughout the match, with one in particular all but wrapping up King’s Row, as it left no survivors while he rode the cart the final meters to victory.


RazzMaTazz : Dps

Although Gopherwatch League is more used to seeing RazzMaTazz holding down the backline on Brig, last match she was too busy peppering the enemy frontline on Soldier 76 instead. The killfeed was lit up by Razz continually finding final blow after final blow as the Skies finished off the Phrogs 3-1 en route to back to back undefeated regular seasons for Razz.


Whalefish : Dps

Reaper guy. Death Blossom go brrrrrr. At this point in the season I think it would be fair to say that Whalefish has killed everyone he has played against so far with a Reaper ult. Whalefish played in 2 matches this week due being traded midway through the week, and both games probably would have landed him a spot on this team. His near deadlift performance once again for the Phrogs carried King’s Row with multi kills on every Blossom, while his presence on the Cats was felt by the Anglers backline all match, with the RNG character hurling logs at their heads over and over.


Yeetmeister : Dps

The Yeetfist when hard this week against the impressive Imps roster, as when I asked some of the Imps members how the game went, I was met with a resounding “Yeet Doomfist” with no other context. The Trout managed to take a map off the Imps, only the second they’ve dropped all season, and the Yeetmeister Doomfist did indeed go hard, disrupting the enemy backline and making each map close.


Maniamoon : Support

Making the team along with her Whales teammate Nort, Maniamoon was integral to the team’s first win this week. With the team down their primary flex support player and no ringer to replace them, Maniamoon was forced to swap to Moira for a few maps. In a 1-0 King’s Row victory, she finished with over 20,000 healing and more damage than the enemy supports combined, while on Blizzard World she nearly ended with more assists than all other supports combined and managed to glock diff Nyamonya’s Cassidy.


RiddleDog : Support

Wrapping up our team this week is RiddleDog, hot off his game against the Pusheens, who they took to map 5. His Brigitte on Dorado likely won his team the map, as he built endless rallies and was near impossible to kill for the Pusheens dive attack. He ran main support for most of the match and was always making big impact plays, especially on Lucio on Lijiang Gardens and Temple of Anubis, where the speed boost ran over the Pusheens first point defense, even with a mishap with a lamp.
