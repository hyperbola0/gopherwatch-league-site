1. Iceland Imps | ±0

Nothing new to say about the Imps honestly. They’re match against the Troubling Trout in Week 5 does not matter for either team, as the Trout only have to care about the Welsh Wales losing to avoid the loser's bracket and the Imps cannot move from their spot.   1. Pokhara Pusheens | +1

The Pusheens proved that their success is not just an under the radar beatdown of the lower teams as they beat the surging Anglers in a 4 map series. PurpleJesus looked the best I have seen him since he still actually played Overwatch and Mikey proved that he still has the Season 3 MVP in him, but the true highlight of this series was Nqthyn’s (mostly) Cassidy play. So long as they don’t get 3-0d by the Arizona O-Teas in Week 5, the Pusheens will secure the #2 seed in the Maroon Division going into playoffs to play the #3 seed in the Gold Division, who could be the Ducks, Phrogs, or even the Skies. 


~ Rusty


  1. Ema Skies | -1

The Ema Skies encountered their first counter composition in their division from the Welsh Whales and they started off struggling, going down 2-0. While that lowered their power rankings spot, they still managed to fight back, adapt, and reverse sweep the Whales to win the series 3-2 and remain undefeated on the season. However, following the Phrogs 3-0 victory over the Trout and their close series against the Whales, their map differential is close enough that the Skies have to beat the Phrogs to keep the #1 seed in the Gold Division. A loss to the Phrogs have them losing their #1 seed and could have them dropping as low as #3 depending on the map score for the Ducks vs. Whales match. What looked like the Skies cruising to a #1 seed clinch has slowly turned into a bit of sweating going into the final week of the season.


~ Rusty


  1. Awesome Anglers | ±0

It was a well fought loss for the Anglers, but not well enough to allow them any breathing room going into the last week of the season. They were able to overtake the Pusheens on Route 66 thanks to the 100% map winrate BOON1E Roadhog (somehow) but were unable to translate that success for the rest of the series. Still, they were competitive against the now #2 team according to power rankings and likely the #2 team in their division. Though our power rankings don’t expect them to lose, any loss, regardless of map differential, results in them starting in the loser's bracket after the loss of their +3 map differential due to their punishment for running an over SR lineup in the series against the O-Teas. They’re hoping it doesn’t come back to bite them.


~ Rusty

  1. Arizona O-Teas | +1

The O-Teas swept the Mighty Ducks this week, although they didn’t really look like themselves. That makes this team hard to rank too, as this would have potentially been a good mid tier matchup that solidified the two in the power rankings. So we still think the Ducks are better than the O-Teas, but it is entirely possible that they aren’t and 4 of the 5 best teams in the league are all in the Maroon division. The O-Teas have a solid lineup, and have looked progressively better each week, so a playoff run is not out of the question. The three R’s continue to look good, as well as Strud3l being a steal in the draft as a 5th round pickup who will definitely get some All Pro votes this season. The O-Teas can finish as high as 2nd in the division with a sweep of the Pusheens, but a more likely outcome is a 3rd or 4th place finish. The Anglers are 3 maps behind them currently and don’t hold the tiebreaker, (Oopsie) but the Anglers get the Cats while the O-Teas get the Pusheens.


~ Nuttyr


  1. Phiji Phrogs | +1

The Phrogs are a tough team to rank. They are now 3-1 and looked really good for the first time this week against the Trout. On the other hand they have had the easiest schedule in the league by far, played most teams at their worst points, and the last match might have been more on the Trout underperforming than the Phrogs overperforming. Nevertheless, Whalefish is looking like he might get his second 1st team All Pro for Dps this season, as his Reaper/Hanzo are currently untouchable. Realistically their match against the Skies will give a better picture of how good this team really is. Until then, they move up a spot for 3-0’ing a struggling team, with the potential to crack the top half next week with a win and can actually win their division as well with a win, as they will have the tiebreaker even with a 3-2 win since they won 3-0, barring a Ducks sweep. (In the case where the Phrogs win 3-2 over the Skies and the Ducks win 3-1 over the Whales, resulting in a three way tie for first place, the Phrogs win the resulting tiebreaker, followed by the Skies. The Ducks loss to the O-Teas may actually result in them going from 1st to 3rd once the dust settles.)

Team of the week 4


    With one week to go in the regular season, we have our first repeat Team of the Week performance, with a player making it in for the second time, but on a different role. We have 3 Supports for the first time this season, and our first ever ringer performance. 4 players on the team won their match this week, with 2 of them winning in style, 3-0. As the season winds down, these players put on their best performances to give their teams the best chance at success in the playoffs trying to either clinch the top spot, or avoid the loser’s bracket starting spot.


Lol247 : Tank

Starting off this week is Lol247’s second appearance on Team of the Week, this team on Tank instead of Dps like in week 1. Slapping around the O-Teas on King’s Row with only 4 players is an impressive feat, even if it wasn’t enough to make up for the Frobel diff. 


Nashbrowns : Tank

As the Imps continue to dominate the league, they took another 3-0 victory this week, this time against the Cats. Nashbrowns took home a match MVP, and ended up having a pop off match. Showing excellent discipline on peels for his Supports and Dps on Sigma and D.Va, Nashbrowns continues to have an excellent rookie season on the best team in the league.


Nqthyn : Dps

Getting constant picks for the Pusheens all match, Nqthyn was a force to be reckoned with. Opening up Blizzard World attack by getting 2 from high ground, getting 5 with a Window High Noon on King’s Row, and hard marking a GM Doomfist for multiple maps, Nqthyn was a key factor in the Pusheens 3-1 win over the Anglers, in what was made out to be one of the closest and consequential matches of the regular season.


Akua : Dps

In what some expected to be a rout, the Welsh Whales looked like they were gonna flip the script, and seemed to have the Ema Skies all figured out. Instead, Akua led the charge on a reverse sweep, switching to Dps for the final 3 maps and winning all of them in order to keep the perfect season alive for the Skies. Akua consistently got fight opening picks and multikills to seal deals, as he kept lighting up the kill feed as Cassidy, with many 2k Noons on Lijiang Gardens to win the match.


Neopaladious : Support

Neopaladious got too many boops to count this week, but capped it off with a double on Lijiang Control Center. His team might have lost to the Ema Skies after being up 2-0, but they were down their primary Flex Support and still held strong in the backline, with a considerable amount of offense being produced from the Main Support’s boop ability.


Phroodles : Support - Ringer

Speaking of Flex Support ringers for the Whales, allow me to introduce Phroodles. With the Whales figuring out that double shield was the way to beat the Skies, Phroodles’ Baptiste came in clutch, dishing out damage while generally outhealing the lobby, and somehow finding it within himself not to int, keeping a low death rate throughout the match.


MuffinLumps : Support

MuffinLumps was clicking for the Phrogs this week, and it really looked like the Phrogs finally figured out how to best utilize him. The team peeled for him on Baptiste, and Muffin did an excellent job of keeping his Tanks alive, especially on King’s Row, with some excellently timed lamps and some good Amplification Matrixes as well. After being more known for their Lucio play, MuffinLumps has been looking like a prime option for Baptiste too.
