Hello and welcome to the first week’s Power Rankings and Team of the Week. After a week of the preds getting nuked match after match, here’s a rough estimate of how the teams stand, but before we get started, there a couple things to keep in mind:


  • This is just Week 1, there is still time for teams to build synergy and figure out compositions

  • We are not an almighty authority that knows everything

  • Balance patch on the 25th could change “meta” and affect team ranking


  1. Mill City Marshmallow Monsters 

Started off with a dominant showing over the Wendigos. Running the perceived meta compositions and Poi absolutely dominating on Zarya for the entire series, they looked unstoppable (inb4 this update somehow manages to screw this team over lol). 


  1. Wensleydale Wendals 

The Wendals had a different kind of dominance over the Pet Rocks, opting for more of a coordinated dive with popoff potential from Akua and TeddyEm. They showed flexibility starting Push with Zarya and finishing Kings Row and Watchpoint: Gibraltar with D.Va to quickly win fights. 


  1. Buenos Aires Beebles 2 

After a rocky start the second iteration of the Beebles figured out what worked for them. Matching Zarya comps all game is a scary strat to do against Fancy, but Cathect looked like he was right back in his prime. Whalefish popping off always helps things, and the Suzu Blossom combo worked wonders for them to clutch the last map.


  1. Handsome Hogs 

In a week filled with pred destroying matches, no game was a bigger bracket buster than this one. A predicted 12-0 ended up as a convincing win for the Hogs, as they completed New Queens Street in 3:02 without giving up so much as a centimeter of progress. The other maps were closer but the Hogs looked like the more coordinated team and took the series convincingly.


  1. Les Chats de Chambord 

Yes this team lost, and yes they still looked good. Fancy showed off how dominant a GM off tank can be in a D.Va Zarya meta, and the team looked coordinated. Their loss isn’t a bad one, as they played the Beebles 2 very close in the 5 map series. Less flanking Zen than I expected, but still a fair amount. 


  1. Casablancan Cappy Blappies

The Cappy Blappies started off strong with a great Push showing against the Bricks and finished off with a good Busan game. They ran some different comps from the rest of the league, taking a chaotic Rein mirror with flexible DPS choices to show that they’re willing to try different things to succeed even if it’s a little unconventional.


  1. Canadian Crewmates 

The Crewmates fought a ringer heavy Mammoths team that was difficult to judge where they stand compared to other teams. Their compositions tend to run closer to counter-meta than meta, but they were able to look good once they were given defensive positioning. Questions about Vind’s tank were answered as the former Orisa specialist was able to flex his muscles a bit to play the meta tanks. 


  1. New York City Newts 

A lot of people had this team at #1 post draft, and I can understand why, as I was one of them. Unfortunately it looked like the tank and support line weren’t coordinated enough on engagements, ult timing was off, and they simply got outplayed on some parts. Overall we think the potential is still there for this team to be top tier, but it just hasn’t shown up yet, as the team looks to flesh out it’s identity after looking worse against the Hogs in mostly mirrored meta comps.


  1. Windigo Wendigos 

The Wendigos had a tough match, where their individual skill got rolled over by their lacking coordination and running into the best looking Zarya in Week 1 in Poi. They still showed good flashes of skill and given time to mesh together could form that skill into better target focus and good match wins, but it may take time for them to be able to do that.


  1. Mankato Mammoths 

It’s difficult to judge how the Mammoths performed given that they had as many as 3 ringers for a large chunk of the match due to internet issues. Still, they were able to take New Queens Street against the Crewmates and the actual members of the Mammoths looked solid enough that we’re going to wait on the full roster before making any real judgments on this team. 


  1. Brooklyn Bricks 

The Bricks looked really solid on King’s Row this week, but didn’t seem to have it for the other maps. Zergling had some pop off moments, but the ultimate rotation at times was a bit questionable from the team, which led to some missed opportunities.


  1. Paris Pet Rocks 

Plain and simple, this team might have gotten Akua diffed. One problem after the first game is that the team has 2 really good hitscan players as their Dps, but isn't really playing compositions or taking off angles to compliment their strengths, instead opting to to mirror the other team’s brawl engagements.





Team of the Week


Tank - Poi

Starting the first Overwatch 2 Team of the Week is Poi from the Marshmallow Monsters. Poi’s comfort hero pool of D.Va and Zarya is a boon for the current meta, and it was on full display in the first match. He managed to have 0 deaths on Zarya through the first two maps, and the Wendigos only landed a single final blow on his Zarya for map 3, ending the match with 5 total deaths, and the most damage and elims in the lobby.

Dps - TeddyEm

As far as Rookie debuts go, this was a pretty strong one. Playing mostly Sojourn for the Wendals as they rolled the Pet Rocks, TeddyEm was constantly lighting up the killfeed along with his Dps partner Akua. As there was no Hidden Gems Combine this season, none of the captains knew what they were getting into if they drafted TeddyEm, but after the first match, he seems to longer be a “hidden” gem.

Dps - Whalefish

Another Sojourn performance, Whalefish came in clutch for the Beebles 2 in a hotly contested 3-2 over Les Chats des Chambord. As the Beebles 2 were down 2-1 heading into map 4, Whalefish turned it up to 11, finding headshot after headshot with the railgun, and decimating les Chats. The Beebles ultimately won maps 4 and 5 off the back of his clutch performance, earning him a spot on Team of the Week.

Support - RiddleDog

Last season’s reigning MVP had a solid start to the season for the Wendals, as a bunch of their players had very good looking individual performances. However, Riddle on Baptiste was backbreaking, as the extra Dps player coming from the backline of the Wendals was racking up kills while keeping the team alive, helping them sustain a heavy presence on the offensive, and keeping the pressure on. His decision to Nano boost TeddyEm every single time proved effective at splitting the enemy focus, as both threats at once was enough to overwhelm the opposition.

Support - Transflect

The ping warrior showed up to his 3 am game and did not disappoint. Playing Kiriko for his lone map on Circuit Royal, the Hogs looked dominant and managed to hold the Newts to a first point cap. Transflect went on the offensive while carefully managing his healing to keep his team alive long enough to turn fights. What really stood out was his critical rate on Kiriko, landing an outstanding amount of headshots on fast and mobile flankers, causing the Newts to struggle when pushing the Hogs backline, and creating lots of opportunities for his team  to counterattack.
