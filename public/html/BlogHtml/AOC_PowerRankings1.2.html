Power Rankings

  1. Mill City Marshmallow Monsters (+0)

Simply put, they stomped this week. There’s not much to say about the Monsters that wasn’t said last week, though I have been told to say that Nuttyr had a “huge” performance on Junkrat. Something that I think I had a nightmare of on Halloween. They’re looking to fight what many consider the other best team in the league in the Wendals for week 3 in what will likely be a battle of the titans. 


  1. Wensleydale Wendals (+0)

The Wendals took another impressive 3-0 win this week over les Chats, and even showed off some alternate lineups to boot, with Akua rolling out on Support for Route 66. The carry potential of a GM Genji/Zarya player is still quite obvious in this meta, and the supporting cast around him look like they synergize extremely well together. Next week they play the other 2-0 team to not drop a map yet, so we will see whether or not these rankings have these two teams in the right order, or if the Wendals get the better of the Monsters.


  1. Casablancan Cappy Blappies (+3)

The Blappies looked much stronger this week than they did in week 1. Their 3-0 victory over the previously 1-0 Crewmates showed that this team is a legitimate contender this season, and the flexible roster is starting to harden into a mold, with Trey playing mostly Dps and Flambo on support. Yoda is looking like a strong option for Rookie of the Year so far, looking incredibly comfortable leading a team, and playing well above his listed sr.


  1. Handsome Hogs (+0)

The Hogs went the full 5 maps in their win over the Bricks this week, making them the 4th team to advance to 2 wins so far. This was probably the closest match of the week, as even when the Hogs were up 2-0, it really felt like anyone’s game. However, good teams find ways to win, and the Poke EJ! synergy so far has worked. It looks like this team is very well coordinated throughout, and should be a solid group that will be tough to beat.


  1. Windigo Wendigos (+4)

After their week 1 loss, the Wendigos bounced back with a statement win, beating the previously 1-0 Beebles 2 in a gentleman’s sweep. After losing a close Ilios map, Typhoon’s Pharah was unstoppable, as the Beebles simply had no answer for it. It also looked like the Wendigos finally found something that works for them, after looking somewhat lost in week 1. Overall the team looked quite strong and as a result find themselves rocketing up the rankings.


  1. Buenos Aires Beebles 2 (-3)

The Beebles had a tough loss this week to the Wendigos, after a promising start on Ilios. The Beebles looked like they did week 1 to start out, with good ultimate rotations and pop off play from their Dps line. However, once they got to Numbani they simply had no answer for Typhoon’s Pharah. After map 2, it just went downhill, as the Beebles made it competitive but didn’t look like the squad we saw in week 1. They look to bounce back this week against the Pet Rocks, who probably won’t be running any Pharah, but have a very high SR Dps line as well.


  1. New York City Newts (+1)

The Newts went a full 5 against the Mammoths, but this match never felt close for either team, as each map was a blowout for one team. Overall the team looked a lot more comfortable then they did in week 1, with much better rotations and positioning from both the front and backlines, but the 2 map losses that weren’t very close is still a bit worrying. Regardless, a win is a win, and the Newts pick up their first one no matter how many maps it took.


  1. Les Chats de Chambord (-3)

After a good looking loss that went the distance in week 1, our underdog protagonists had an uphill battle against the undefeated on maps Wendals. Unfortunately, it took les Chats until map 3 to figure out something that worked against the Wendals. Once they did, Route 66 was an excellent map, with both teams running somewhat off meta comps, and les Chats on comfort picks, which they looked much more comfortable on. However, an unfortunate C9 ended the series in a fight that les Chats were poised to win, so they will have to wait another week to break the curse, because of course PapaTriste was ringing again.


  1. Brooklyn Bricks (+2)

The Bricks fall to 0-2, but they looked a lot better overall, and seem like they made vast improvements, even when down SR due to having 2 ringers. They went the distance against a strong Hogs squad, and almost had the reverse sweep after being down 2-0. Even when they lost the first 2 maps, this team was fighting tooth and nail with the Hogs, and made each map close, with Ilios coming down to wire on every single point. Overall this team looked like they made really good strides in improving as a team, and should pick up a win sooner or later, as their match next week against the Newts should be a very close match.


  1. Canadian Crewmates (-3)

While the Crewmates record would indicate a higher placement on the rankings, the team did not look like a cohesive unit against the Cappy Blappies, and really didn’t have anything against their counterpicks of Echo and Sombra. Obviously this team isn’t the most flexible, with only 2 legal lineups and a very limited hero pool, but this week it hurt them and it showed in the match result.


  1. Mankato Mammoths (-1)

The Mammoths had their first round pick this week but were unable to translate it into a win. They looked very dominant on their 2 map wins, but lackluster on the losses. Trading for Goseek does make a lot of sense for this team, and it should make them a stronger team overall, as it opens up a lot of options for them to have another tank player for a team that needs another one in order to run a second roster.


  1. Paris Pet Rocks (+0)

Unfortunately the Pet Rocks had to go up against the Marshmallow Monsters this week with their first and second round picks (both DPS) being rung for. It wasn’t really possible to make a note of their performance one way or the other but they look to battle back against a Beebles 2 team that lost to the Wendigos last week (hopefully with their full roster this time). 


Team of the Week

Tank : Yoda

Starting off Team of the Week is the rookie tank for the Cappy Blappies. Looking dominant on any tank pick throughout their win over the Crewmates, Yoda led the team in a convincing win, and had some huge point winning ultimates on King’s Row, getting a 4 man Grav to secure first point.


Dps : Typhoon

Typhoon’s Pharah single handedly picked apart the Beebles on Numbani, as so far in the season his Pharah has been the brightest off meta pick for any Dps, consistently building barrage in under a minute, sometimes even under 30 seconds.


Dps : Guku

The Mei pick, although banned now, was doing some serious work on King’s Row in conjunction with Poke’s Reinhardt, making it very difficult for the Bricks to take aggressive positioning, as her walls with Reinhardt's close range pressure combined to disrupt the Bricks comp enough to open up massive holes for Mightydwarf on Reaper.


Supp : Frosty

Coming up with 2 huge clutch moments this week was Frosty, grabbing himself a Play of the Game on Ilios to boot. In addition to his 3k on Moira in the Ruins of Ilios, Frosty had an incredibly clutch sleep dart on the Mammoths Tracer at the end of second point Havana, killing them and then stalling long enough for his 4 teammates to come back and win the fight, ending the attack from the Mammoths.


Supp : Flipflop

Rounding out our Team of the Week is FlipFlop, who’s Kiriko play was very consistent at keeping her Dps and Tank up all match, and getting the better of the opposite Kiriko in the 1v1s. A few clutch Suzu’s were enough to flipflop multiple fights, and the Kitsune Rush’s usually resulted in team fight wins, with a few flipping lost fights.
