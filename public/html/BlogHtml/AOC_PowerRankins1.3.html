Power Rankings


  1. Mill City Marshmallow Monsters

MCMM remains the team to beat taking out #1 spot in the power rankings. After absolutely stomping the Wendals, they cemented their spot as the best team of the Borealis division and for that they kept their top spot on our power rankings. Simply put, the team stomped and will likely continue to stomp unless the November 15th patch drastically changes the meta. 


  1. Casablancan Cappy Blappies 

Coming out with another strong week, the Cappy Blappies find themselves at the top spot in the Australis Division in both our rankings and the standings. Trey was a menace on Genji against the Mammoths, who had no real answer for his nanoblades, as he decimated the backline. After a few hiccup plays in week 2 that still resulted in a 3-0 victory, this squad also looked a lot cleaner this week, minimizing mistakes and coordinating very well. Their match next week against the Handsome Hogs will likely determine the winner of their division, something to watch for.


  1. Handsome Hogs

The Hogs rolled once again this week, with Poke running over the Crewmates in a clean 3-0 sweep. The coordination as a fast paced brawl intensive team has looked really good so far for this team, and that isn’t even considering the eventual Winston meta coming up, which Poke is well known for playing to a very high degree.


  1. Windigo Wendigos 

Coming in at number 4 is the Windigo Wendigos, who have pulled off two solid wins in a row now, and having our 4th out of 5 3-0 victories on the week. The Zarya Pharah comp is really starting to look like it will cement itself as this teams bread and butter, as Typhoon and Corey are really connecting on Bubble Barrages (™) and posing a considerable threat to enemy teams, as it creates a paradox of choice for them: either run D.Va and hitscan to deal with the Pharah and get run over by the Zarya, or match the Zarya and take a beating from the Pharah. Either one is a lose lose scenario, which is why the Wendigos find themselves on a 2 week win streak.


  1. Wensleydale Wendals 

It was a tough week for the Wendals. Going against the best team in the league, the coordination and teamfight sweeping from Weeks 1 and 2 were gone, with Akua playing only 2 minutes of his signature Genji, swapping to other characters he felt more comfortable with that day. Still, losing to the best team in the power rankings shouldn’t be punished too harshly, so they still sit in the Top 5 due to their previous performances.


  1. Buenos Aires Beebles 2

With the fifth and final 3-0 victory this week, the Beebles 2 pulled one off against a struggling Pet Rocks team. The team looked good, but the next 2 weeks will be the make or break moment for this team, as they play against the Marshmallow Monsters and the Wendals, which should be a tough match for each team involved.


  1. Brooklyn Bricks 

The Bricks reigned in the new era of a post trade Brooklyn Bricks with a close Map 5 victory over their New York counterpart the New York City Newts. Though they faltered on Rialto and Oasis, their hot start to the series saved them long enough to conquer the newts on New Queen Street. Cupcake also made his season debut and it’s unfortunate that he was unable to show up until now because he performed extremely well and I hope he can continue to do well for them. 


  1. New York City Newts 

The Newts have had a tough go of it this season, but their ability to keep long series has allowed them to remain as a middle of the pack team. They haven’t been able to firmly close out any games other than their win vs. the Mammoths. Still, they didn’t go down without a fight, bringing it to Map 5 in convincing fashion before falling just short. Based on the comps they’ve been running, they’re waiting for the November 15th patch to truly blossom. 


  1. Les Chats de Chambord 

This team at nine is a crime. Les Chats de Chambord is the team that has the highest potential in the league. They may not have put up the results yet, but that's because they don’t want to. They are playing to catch people off guard, this week is going to be a completely reinvigorated Chats team. Fancy is the best tank in the league, Fli is a god Genji gamer, Cent is a Pharah who should be able to out-maneuver the Dps of the Pet Rocks, and Paige has been doing extremely good this season through her Mercy and Lucio gameplay, as well as BBQ on the flex support introducing a potential change in play style. Personally, I’m predicting a swift 3-0 for Les Chats de Chambord, breaking the Paige team curse and proving the league wrong. In my heart and in my brain this team is in first and will make a run through the playoffs and win this season.


  1. Canadian Crewmates 

Down their second round pick in WiggleHiggle, the Crewmates were on the backfoot this week against a surging Hogs squadron. Their anti-meta playstyle just didn’t find enough of a footing to disrupt the Hogs fast paced blitz, and as a result fell short of a map win. This team will look to bounce back next week against the Newts, as one team will make it back to the .500 mark.


  1. Mankato Mammoths 

Unfortunately for the Mammoths, they had a bit of a nightmare matchup this week, as the team struggled to deal with a Genji throughout the whole match. Interestingly enough, this team has made a trade, picking GoSeek as a secondary or primary Tank option, which should shore up some meta slots for the team to try new things going forward.


  1. Paris Pet Rocks 

The Pet Rocks had another rough week, but the team did look better despite the loss. It was forced to run PurpleJesus on support instead of Dps, so the team wasn’t really at full strength. Next week they match up well against les Chats, which presents an interesting opportunity as the double hitscan Pet Rocks play a Pharah centric Chats squad.



Team of the Week

Tank: Poke1236ok

Coming out with another dominant performance this week was Poke, whose Zarya proved to be too much to handle for the Crewmates, who much prefer the D.Va comps. Poke ran it down on Colosseo, cranking out huge Gravs and multikills, and asserting what he has all season, that he is one of the league’s best Zarya players at the moment.


Dps: Seven7 

Fortune favors the bold, and Seven7 showed no hesitation this week in matching Akua’s Genji. It paid off for him, coming out with play of the game in both maps he played, including a shutdown of an enemy Dragonblade with his own. 


Dps: Cupcake

Matching up against Mikey this week was Cupcake, and as the two battled it out the entire match, both were popping off and making huge plays. Ultimately Cupcake and the Bricks prevailed, as his clutch performance late in the match resulted in a swift end to a reverse sweep attempt by the Newts, and knocking the Bricks for match W in the standings.


Support: Strud3l 

Strud3l had one of the best performances for a support this season against the Wendals, being virtually unkillable throughout the match. Gibraltar is where he truly shined, with multiple key Suzus to save his teammates, and hitting headshot after headshot, racking up the kills.


Support: Salty

Salty was super consistent this week on Ana, hitting sleeps left and right, and staying alive throughout, exactly what you want to see from your Flex Support. Another rookie on the Team of the Week, Salty has quietly been one of the better Flex Supports this season, as his consistency has been nigh unmatched, being a key reason for the Hogs success.
