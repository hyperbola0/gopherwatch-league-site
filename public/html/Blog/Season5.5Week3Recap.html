Gopherwatch League Season 5.5 Week 3 Power Rankings

Iceland Imps | ±0 
 

Another week of domination for the Imps as they collect another 3-0, clutching out fights and keeping their cool to sweep the O-Teas after flirting with losses on Busan and King’s Row. Still, they bounced back and absolutely dominated on Junkertown to finish out what looked to be a tough series against the #8 team according to Week 2’s power rankings. Trey* finally met his match on the Reinhardt, a more aggressive Reinhardt that was willing to int more than him in Rusty (the writer), who had more deaths than Trey had finals blows on King’s Row, yet still went to Overtime to finish their win on attack.   1. Ema Skies | ±0

The Skies were able to hold back a surging Anglers team this week in the closest interdivision match of the season. They hit a few walls in the series, notably getting dominated on Rialto, but came back on Hollywood and closed it out to come out on top in Map 5. To say the Fancy Reinhardt was less than optimal is an understatement, but the Skies realized this and adjusted their playstyle for the rest of the series to avoid using it in any situation and it worked, looking great on all of their counterpick maps and even using double shield on Lijiang Tower: Control Center. Based on how the rest of their schedule looks, they should be able to finish the season undefeated as they run into the Whales fresh off two Map 5 series next week and enter the Phrog Zone in Week 5. 


~ Rusty


  1. Pokhara Pusheens | ±0 Rusty

In the Battle of the Felines, the Pusheens prevailed as they 3-0’d the Chicago Cats, playing stronger than any team the Cats had faced this season. Mikey wanted to show off that he could still dominate a lobby, getting 89% kill participation on Busan. They cruised for the rest of the series, with a quick repeat of Numbani attack to win in Round 3 and a coasting finish on Dorado to assert dominance as the superior Cats of Season 5.5. There wasn’t anything during the match that should indicate any shift in their current spot on the power rankings, but their Week 4 match against the Anglers will be the game that decides whether they’re a top team or a gatekeeper.

The Trout looked off their game against the Ducks this week. There were some interesting comp choices on every map, and I don’t think any of them worked in their favor. Team of the Week 3


    This week featured some excellent performances, and oddly enough, only 3 of our 7 Team of the Week players won their matches this week. There were some incredible performances by players from each role this week that might have gone overlooked since they did not take home a win for the week, but they did not go unnoticed. There were a variety of reasons for each of the 7 picks this week, from showing dominance on Main Tank to the shield that defends the team from certain defeat, from carry performances on Dps to coming in clutch on an off role when the team needed it most, from playing a consistent backline all match long to coming in late in a match and shutting down the opposition to clinch a win, here are our 7 picks this week for Team of the Week…


BOON1E : Tank

Starting us off this week is the former commissioner and host of the Emmy nominated hit show BOON1ESPN, BOON1E. The match between the Skies and Anglers was possibly the best thriller of the season so far, and the battle between Fancy and BOON1E on Reinhardt was exquisite. Although Fancy on Reinhardt probably isn’t where the team normally wants them, you couldn’t deny them the 1v1, which BOON1E won convincingly. He made huge plays throughout the match, mostly as Reinhardt, including a few very clutch shatters that sealed the deal on maps like Busan Downtown and MEKA Base. Ultimately the Anglers came up just short of the victory against the undefeated Skies, but BOON1E was probably too busy asserting the Main Tank diff to notice.


RustyGoomba : Tank

Joining his buddy this week is Rusty, for another Reinhardt performance. Most of the time when we see great performances from Reinhardt’s it’s all about how much damage they were putting out, how many elims they got, and how many fat shatters they landed. Not this time. RustyGoomba makes our Team of the Week for his King’s Row against the devastating Iceland Imps. Although Rusty may have a reputation as Reinhardt to be very aggressive and int in to make space, this week his contribution to the O-Teas was shown in how well he handled the pressure from the Imps aggressive assaults from Trey and Invictus. Rusty showed the correct way to handle an aggressive Reinhardt by giving space when necessary and opening up his Reaper to make plays in the backline while trusting his Baptiste to keep him up, and it almost handed the Imps their second map loss of the season, despite being over 150 sr lower as a team, down one of their supports.


Jack : Dps

Jack got to show off his flexibility this week, playing a couple of characters that only a select few Dps play in GWL, most notably Sombra and Pharah. His Sombra gameplay in coordination with his team resulted in some very clutch teamfight wins on Hollywood. His Pharah then carried the team on Lijiang Control Center, outpacing Bonezee on Tracer by grabbing multikill after multikill, in an eventual map 5 win.


Zelos : Dps

Offroling for an Anglers squad down Mightydwarf, Zelos came up big for his team. Looking good on a variety of heroes, especially Symmetra, Soldier, and Reaper, Zelos had a huge impact on a secondary role in an extremely close series against a top team.


Whalefish : Manb

Whalefish had perhaps the single most dominant match performance by a player this season on Dps against the Whales. 72 elims as Reaper on KR, aimbotting on Hanzo on multiple maps, huge multi-picks to open fights, and consistent play throughout the match, Whalefish was a one man army in the Phrogs first divisional match win.


EJ! : Support

The Trout had a rough match this week, getting 3-0d by the Ducks. EJ! was a lone bright spot however, playing a very consistent game in his first match as a full time starter. His Lucio on King’s Row impressed, and he managed to stay alive at a high rate on most heroes, maximizing his healing uptime and getting a few big plays to keep his team in the fight.


Frosty : Support

Frosty made his first appearance of the season in the Pusheens first divisional win against the Cats, after internet troubles and scheduling had him miss the first two matches. He didn’t miss a beat, locking down the Cats dive tanks on Brig and keeping the team alive while the Cats snipers picked apart his backline. Frosty even picked up a PotG, giving him a 100% map winrate and PotG rate on the season, in addition to his dominant performance.
