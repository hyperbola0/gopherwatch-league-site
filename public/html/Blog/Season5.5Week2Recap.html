Gopherwatch League Season 5.5 Week 2 Power Rankings


    This week saw some shake ups at the lower portion of the rankings, while the top 4 teams from last week faced each other. With the Anglers once again being big movers, other teams also shifted around quite a bit, especially our aquatic based teams that the Anglers will be fishing for come playoffs. The Anglers vs O-Teas was a 3-0, but will count as 0-0 in the map differential, but other than that match, there were some good games this week, including a 3-2 nailbiter between the Cats and the Phrogs. The Imps and Skies still lead their divisions and look like the teams to beat, and with their victories over our 3rd and 4th place teams, it might stay that way come playoffs. With that out of the way, let's jump in for our 1st team…


  1. Iceland Imps | ±0 

The Iceland Imps have yet to be dethroned from their #1 place on our power rankings, as they managed a comfortable victory against the Pusheens this week. The power of the Imps line-up is well-known by now, as their core is undeniably strong and their lower SR players (Nashbrowns, Charbotron, and War) constantly overperform. In this match in particular, however, the Imps demonstrated that they’re not just a stacked roster. Their ability to make immediate switches to their teamplay and compositions allowed them to break parity in the neutral, and come back stronger after lost fights. Furthermore, the clear synergy between Main Tank and Captain Syn and Flex Support Sly (the legendary Bark & Meow combo) was nothing less than oppressively dominant on Kings Row, allowing the Imps to stifle the Pusheens in spawn and (with extraordinary heroics from Invictus and FedoraMaster in a 2v4 situation) close out their defense without the Pusheens capturing first point. Until someone can figure out the secret to stumping the Imps, this team will remain the Kings of the Power Rankings.


~lostsilver


  1. Ema Skies | ±0 

The Skies won against the Ducks last week with a convincing 3-1 win. Fancy looked like they were smurfing on Roadhog for most of the match. When Fancy is on tank, this team wins. When Fancy is not on tank, this team does not win. As Fancy could potentially miss some games later in the season or come playoffs, this could be problematic for the Skies, but at 2-0 with a +4 map differential, this team has all but secured a playoff spot. The team looks very reminiscent of the Hawks as they like to run the Hog with an anchor tank, but doesn’t seem to be as dominant on rush and double shield as the Hawks were, instead opting for annoying poke or Sombra comps instead to create opportunities for Fancy and Jack to pop off. Although they beat a good team, they do not move up our power rankings to the number one spot, as the Imps managed to beat a team ranked even higher than the Ducks.


~Nuttyr


  1. Pokhara Pusheens | ±0 

The Pusheens lost 3-1 this week, holding their place as they found the Imps weak point, giving them their first map loss on a map that originally looked favorable to the Imps. A special appearance by Veralidaine, season 4’s best captain winner, was with the Pusheens as their ringer, reuniting with PurpleJesus once again. Kings Row was questionable, having an unexpected turnout. Eventually PurpleJesus brought out the Godrisa, carrying his team through Havana. Ribro and Nuttyr did well but it wasn't enough to hold out on Volskaya, and they were unable to capture point B as Invictus on the high ground was quite deadly. Nqthyn got picks and made miracles happen but they still couldn't keep the momentum going with it being shut down by both Bob and Molten Core. Next week the Pusheens play the (Cursed) Chicago Cats. Even with the prediction to win I think it could be close. The Pusheens might need to be worried about an upset. 


~Yeetmeister


  1. Dorado Ducks | ±0 

The Ducks looked good when they had control over the space and were able to pick and choose their engages, picking apart mistakes made by the Skies on Ilios with their unorthodox Roadhog and Winston compositions. Unfortunately, the team struggled with Sombra, and as a result weren’t able to find their engagements as easily for the rest of the series. Still, they looked strong against one of the best teams in the league, so there isn’t much of a reason to lower them in these power rankings. As of this week’s Power Rankings, their match against the Trout in Week 3 could be the closest match of next week, and will solidify the placement of both the Trout and the Ducks in next week’s Power Rankings. 


~ Rusty


  1. Awesome Anglers | +5 

The Awesome Anglers played quite well, coming off a 0-3 and onto a 3-0 against the O-Teas. BOON1E pulled his team up showing off his Winston which he’s not well known for,  allowing Bonezee and MightyDwarf to (tactically) feed their brains out. I would say that Sonicronic is probably the Awesomest of Anglers, keeping his team protected and staying consistent, all while keeping up with RustyGoomba on D.Va. The Anglers support line also kept up with arguably some of the best Supports in the league. Kirbology and Zelos saved lives with both well timed beats and immortality fields. Kirbology notably had 10,000 less damage than Strudel on Kings Row. I think the Anglers have the potential to be a top tier team and I’m glad to see them come back from week one looking stronger. Next week the Anglers play the Ema Skies. Will Bonzee be able to take on Akua? Will BOON1E be able to deal with the hog wild Fancy? One thing is for sure, that nothing is off the table just yet.  


~ Yeetmeister

 

  1. Troubling Trout | +2 

Given the extenuating circumstances of the Trout’s Week 1 game, predicting the outcome of their Week 2 matchup seemed like an impossible task. Luckily for these little fish, the Trout seemed to find their footing and didn’t flounder against the #9 Welsh Whales this week. The DPS line for the Trout came alive in this match, with RektCactus popping off on his signature Cassidy on Kings Row and Liftkey blowing expectations out of the water with his Hanzo gameplay. Furthermore, Orion’s tank flexibility allowed for Yeetmeister to make a splash on his old role of Main Tank, and the supportline of EJ! and Zukini contributed to their team’s flashy plays and team synergy swimmingly. Overall this team looked much more solid and comfortable all-around this week, earning them the #6 spot in our power rankings after clearly demonstrating their ability to play to one another’s strengths. Going into Week 3, the Trout will face the #4 Dorado Ducks in a decisive series which is sure to leave the viewers hooked! :)


~ lostsilver


  1. Phiji Phrogs | ±0 

The Phrogs took a 3-2 win over the Cats this week, but it isn’t enough to move them up the charts. The team tried some new things, and actually did better with Phrog on dps, winning the final 2 maps from a 2-1 deficit. However, blown opportunities on both sides could’ve easily turned this into a 3-0, but some heroics from Whalefish saved this team multiple times, as he narrowly misses out on Team of the Week. The fact that this team very nearly got 3-0d by our bottom team worries me, but at the same time, the resilience and adaptability in their comeback makes me think this team is still figuring out how to get the most value out of their roster. This team could upset some teams, and could be upset by anyone, as they reside in the purgatory of the Phrog zone. In the Phrog zone every match is both winnable and losable, but as far as playoffs go, it's better to have a high ceiling and be inconsistent  than to be consistently average. 


-Nuttyr


  1. Arizona O-Teas | -2

The O-Teas took an unfortunate 3-0 to the 10th ranked Anglers, but to be fair to the O-Teas, the Anglers are clearly better than 10, now that we’ve seen some more from them. Other than Dorado, the maps were close, and losing to an Anglers roster that had a pop off performance by multiple players isn’t the end of the world, but their next match is against the Imps, so it won’t get any easier. Riddle put on an excellent performance on Ilios Well, but it wasn’t enough, as the team wasn’t able to sustain long enough against Anglers aggressive play. Overall, the week wasn’t an absolute disaster, as the map differential for the match gets nullified, and when only 1 team misses playoffs in a 4 match season, that's almost as good as a win. Even if this team loses the next two divisional games, they should make the playoffs, as they hold the tiebreaker over the Cats in the head to head, and are +1 in map differential. A 3-0 always sucks, but not losing anything in the map differential department might make all the difference, so the O-Teas can at least sleep well on a likely playoff berth, despite finding themselves near the bottom of the rankings.


-Nuttyr


  1. Welsh Whales | -4 

The Whales started off this week with a problem, scheduling. To compensate, they got Gopherwatch League Alumni Kobalt to play his first matches of non-sleep deprived midnight Overwatch quick play in over a year and a half as a ringer (source: me). They won the control map, but struggled to settle in for the rest of the series, aside from a “literal cock torture” of an Eichenwalde draw in overtime before eventually ending the series in a 3-1 loss on Map 5. The tank switching continues to be a bit of a struggle, but it looks like they’re having TheNortLogic settle in on Main Tank with Corey and Centurion doing some role switching to let everyone on the team get equivalent playing time. The Whales have a match against the Phiji Phrogs next week, which if their track record and power rankings placement holds up, could put them in another 5 map slog. The torture isn’t over yet. 


~ Rusty


  1. Chicago Cats | -1 

The Curse strikes again. This team was oh so close to notching a win against the Phrogs, which would break the curse, thus rocketing them up the power rankings. Alas, their 2-1 lead dissipated as they were unable to put much together on maps 4 and 5, and a questionable attack strategy on King’s Row cost them that map, as they were winning in the head to head brawl on defense. ETC popped off on various hitscans, but unfortunately for the Cats, they were unable to finish the Phrogs while down Cathect. Next week they take on the Pusheens, who despite their high ranking are only 0-1 in the division, so the Cats could make up some major ground with a win and move up the standings despite their lackluster start.


-Nuttyr



Team of the week

Rektcactus : Dps

RektCactus helped the Trout to their first win of the season, as he popped off on Cassidy on multiple maps. With plenty of multikills in the match, finding highlights this week shouldn’t be a problem. The team even ran Shanghai comp for a bit, and even while battling with disconnect issues Rektcactus was still able to stand out as the team’s go to hitscan.

Zukini : Support

With several key sleep darts thrown at Corey this week, Zukini finds herself on totw by making a few game changing plays on Ana, including big nades, well timed sleeps, and clutch kills. The disruption from Zukini’s Ana was enough to flip multiple fights and had a tremendous impact throughout the match.

Riddledog : Support

Riddle wasn’t playing overwatch on Ilios this week. He was playing boop BOON1E into the pit simulator. With Riddle getting a ton of environmental elims on Well, it's a shame his team was not able to win, but that doesn't stop him from earning his place on team of the week.

MightyDwarf : Dps

With a huge play on kings row to cap third point on the reaper guy, killing 4 in an eventual map win, MightyDwarf was Mightydiffing this week for the Anglers. With huge clutch plays and spitting out loads of damage, MightyDwarf gets a 3-0 and a spot on team of the week.

Sonic : Tank

Getting about the same amount of damage and eliminations as  Bonezee’s dps while on off tank is no easy feat, but this week Sonicronic managed to do it in their sweep of the O-Teas. His D.Va was pounding the O-Teas on Ilios, despite his other tank hard throwing by plunging into the well at every possible moment.

Trey : Tank

Trey spent this week’s match terrorizing the Pusheens on brawl and his famous “6 man but with Ball and Torb” comp. Putting the clamps on PurpleJesus on King’s Row, lapping him in shatters 2 minutes in, Trey showed off his flexibility as the main tank for the league’s top team 2 weeks in.

Fancy: Tank

Fancy was simply unfair against the Ducks. They were utterly unstoppable on Roadhog, and it looked like they were smurfing hard. Even on Sigma, Fancy was able to dominate on Hollywood. With inexplicably high damage output this week, Fancy’s off tank was a force to reckoned with in the Ema Skies win. It looks like Fancy’s MVP performances are going to stick around for the foreseeable future
